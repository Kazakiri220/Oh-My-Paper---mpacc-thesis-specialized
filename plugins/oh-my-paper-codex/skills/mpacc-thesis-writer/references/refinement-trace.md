## Cross-Sample Rules Kept

- Case and model theses need an evidence inventory before drafting.
- Excellent structures keep theory/framework/case/problem/solution in a visible chain.
- Digital or AI topics remain MPAcc-appropriate only when technology serves accounting, risk, cost, audit, budget, or performance decisions.
- Strong recommendations are mapped to diagnosed problems and causes.
- Literature reviews are thematic and end in a usable research gap.
- Method-heavy theses must pass method-data fit before drafting.
- Accounting-treatment theses require economic substance, standard mapping, recognition timing, measurement, presentation/disclosure, and case verification.
- Mechanism/effect theses require path variables and effect indicators; event theses separate direct effect from spillover.
- Topic selection must not start from sample availability, method preference, or title fashion; local standards require a visible real professional problem and evidence boundary first.

## Quality Gate

Each rule in the runtime references was checked for:

- specificity: can guide a concrete writing decision;
- operability: includes action, field, table, or test;
- non-copying: contains no source-thesis paragraphs, tables, or original data;
- traceability: abstracted from one or more topic types above rather than a single phrase.
