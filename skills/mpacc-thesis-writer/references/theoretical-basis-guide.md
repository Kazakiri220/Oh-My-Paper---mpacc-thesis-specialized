# Theoretical Basis Guide

Theory is not the same as an analysis framework. Theory explains why relationships or behaviors occur; a framework organizes what to observe or measure.

## Selection Rule

Use 2-4 theories. For each theory, write:

1. Core proposition in one sentence.
2. Why it fits the thesis problem.
3. Where it reappears in case analysis, cause analysis, or recommendations.

## Frequent MPAcc Theories

| Theory | Use For | Later Connection |
|---|---|---|
| Principal-agent theory | governance, internal control, budget responsibility, audit risk | explain information asymmetry, monitoring gaps, incentive misalignment |
| Information asymmetry | credit risk, related-party identification, investor protection, disclosure quality | justify data expansion, disclosure improvement, audit procedures |
| Stakeholder theory | ESG, environmental cost, social responsibility, public funds | map stakeholder demands to indicators or safeguards |
| Resource dependence / resource orchestration | digital transformation, mixed ownership, innovation performance | explain resource acquisition, bundling, capability formation |
| Transaction cost theory | supply chain, related-party transactions, governance mechanism | analyze coordination and control costs |
| Externality theory | environmental cost, carbon accounting, public policy | justify internalizing environmental/social costs |
| Life-cycle theory | engineering projects, carbon cost, full-process cost | organize stages and control points |
| Balanced scorecard / EVA / DuPont | performance evaluation | convert theory into indicators, not a standalone theory section |
| Risk management theory | credit, financial warning, audit, internal control | connect risk identification, assessment, response, monitoring |

## Theory-To-Text Template

`[Theory] suggests [mechanism]. In this thesis, [case object] faces [specific problem], so the theory supports analyzing [chapter/indicator/process] and designing [recommendation].`

Good: "Information asymmetry theory supports expanding A bank's credit risk data from financial statements to tax, transaction, and judicial data, because small firms often lack complete audited statements."

Weak: "Information asymmetry theory is a famous theory and is very important to this thesis."

## Anti-Pattern

Do not stack unrelated theories. If a theory does not guide a later indicator, cause, or solution, remove it.
