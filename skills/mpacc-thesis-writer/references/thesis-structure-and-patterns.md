# Thesis Structure And Patterns

Derived pattern: strong MPAcc theses usually convert a practical accounting/finance problem into a traceable chain: context -> literature gap -> theory/framework -> case evidence -> problem diagnosis -> cause analysis -> improvement plan or effect evaluation -> conclusion.

## Official Form Versus Analytical Pattern

Do not confuse official thesis form with chapter pattern.

Official MPAcc thesis forms include case analysis report, thematic research paper, survey report, scheme design, and product design. The patterns below are analytical structures that help organize the argument after the official form, research question, and evidence boundary are clear.

Before choosing any structure, state:

- official thesis form;
- analytical pattern;
- research question;
- provisional answer/main claim;
- case design and evidence boundary.

## Common Structures

1. Case optimization thesis:
   Introduction; literature and theory; case organization/current practice; problems and causes; optimization scheme and safeguards; conclusion.
   Use for internal control, budget management, cost management, risk management, audit improvement, and performance evaluation.

2. Mechanism and effect thesis:
   Introduction; concept/theory; case/event background; mechanism or path analysis; economic/performance effect; conclusion and suggestions.
   Use for mixed ownership reform, digital transformation, investor protection, resource orchestration, and governance effects.

3. Model or accounting treatment thesis:
   Introduction; concepts/theory; existing rules and pain points; model/framework construction; case application; conclusion.
   Use for data asset recognition, carbon cost accounting, forestry carbon sink accounting, intelligent warning models, and social network identification.

4. Event and spillover thesis:
   Introduction; concepts/theory; event/case facts; direct effect analysis; spillover analysis; conclusion and suggestions.
   Use for litigation, investor protection, default events, disclosure events, and governance shocks.

5. Configuration thesis:
   Introduction; concepts/theory; condition selection; sample and calibration; necessity/configuration analysis; configuration naming and case interpretation; conclusion.
   Use when several conditions combine to produce an outcome, such as mixed reform and CSR performance.

6. Staged path thesis:
   Introduction; theory and literature; case stage division; driver-resource-capability path by stage; effect evaluation; conclusion.
   Use for digital transformation, resource orchestration, and capability evolution.

## Chapter Functions

- Introduction: explain the real problem, why it matters now, why the case is representative, what key factor(s) may explain it, and what practical accounting question the thesis answers.
- Literature/theory chapter: synthesize themes, identify gaps, define concepts, and justify the theory used later.
- Case/status chapter: describe only facts needed for analysis. Do not let company history replace diagnosis.
- Problem/cause chapter: identify concrete symptoms with data, process evidence, interview evidence, or comparison.
- Design/effect chapter: propose a framework, verify with the case, or evaluate effects using financial and non-financial indicators.
- Conclusion: answer the research question, state contributions and limitations, and avoid adding unsupported new claims.

## Title Pattern

Prefer `context/tool + case/object + accounting problem + action`.

Good: `数字化转型背景下 A 制造企业营运资金管理优化研究`

Weak: `企业财务管理问题研究`

Why: the good title exposes context, case, problem, and action; the weak title is too broad and has no evidence boundary.

## Abstract Pattern

Use 5 moves: background urgency; research gap; case and method; main findings or design; contribution and limitation.

Good: "在公开数据、访谈和流程资料基础上，识别 A 公司预算编制中预测偏差、项目评估主观和流程低效三类问题，并构建 RPA+AI 预算编制框架。"

Weak: "本文对预算管理进行了深入研究，并提出了具有重要意义的建议。"

## Quality Gate

Before accepting any structure:

- The chapter order must mirror the research question.
- Each chapter must have a unique job; do not duplicate literature, theory, and analysis.
- The case chapter must feed the diagnosis chapter with evidence.
- The recommendation/effect chapter must answer diagnosed problems rather than append generic management advice.
- The structure must match the thesis type; do not force a model thesis into a problem-cause-solution outline when it needs recognition/measurement/application logic.
- The structure must not become a working report with many parallel problems and measures. Use "point breakthrough, deep proof" for one core problem or mechanism.
