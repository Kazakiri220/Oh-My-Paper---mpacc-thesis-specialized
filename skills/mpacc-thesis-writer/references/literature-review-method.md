# Literature Review Method

The literature review should create a research position, not fill pages.

## Organization

Prefer thematic synthesis:

- concept and measurement studies;
- influencing factors or mechanisms;
- method/model studies;
- case/application studies;
- research gaps and thesis contribution.

Use chronological order only when the thesis studies policy evolution, accounting standards, or technology development.

## Review Paragraph Pattern

1. Topic sentence naming the theme.
2. Group several studies by viewpoint or method.
3. Compare what they explain and miss.
4. Link to this thesis.

Good: "Existing financial-risk-warning studies have moved from single financial ratios to combined financial and text indicators, but industry-specific MD&A tone has not been sufficiently tested in textile and apparel cases."

Weak: "Author A studied X. Author B studied Y. Author C studied Z. In conclusion, many scholars studied this topic."

## Source Balance

- Use the user's provided 40-50 references at runtime.
- Prioritize recent, authoritative, and directly relevant sources.
- For Chinese MPAcc theses, CSSCI/core journals, accounting/audit/finance journals, official policy documents, standards, annual reports, and industry reports are usually stronger than generic web articles.
- Do not invent foreign literature or classic citations.

## Linking To Gaps

Common usable gaps:

- existing studies focus on general listed companies, while this thesis studies a specific industry/case;
- existing models rely only on financial indicators, while this thesis adds text/process/non-financial data;
- existing accounting treatment is conceptual, while this thesis constructs a case-applicable recognition, measurement, presentation, or disclosure path;
- existing governance/effect studies use linear regression, while this thesis uses case mechanism, fsQCA, event study, or process tracing.

Quality gate: each literature theme must support a later part of the thesis; remove themes that never reappear.
