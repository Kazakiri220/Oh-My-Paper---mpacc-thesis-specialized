# Proposal And Literature Review Deliverables

Treat the opening report and standalone literature review as independent deliverables, not fragments copied from the thesis.

## Opening Report Structure

1. Topic background and significance.
2. Domestic and foreign research status.
3. Research content and research questions.
4. Research methods and technical route.
5. Case/data feasibility.
6. Case design and evidence boundary.
7. Expected innovation.
8. Schedule.
9. Reference list.

## Proposal Quality Rules

- Distinguish topic from research question. The proposal must state the visible reality problem, key associated factor(s), consequence, and provisional answer/main claim.
- Pass the hard topic gates in `topic-selection-framework.md` before polishing the title.
- Show feasibility: list available annual reports, standards, interviews, datasets, or process documents.
- Link methods to questions: if using AHP, ML, fsQCA, event study, or social network analysis, state input data and output.
- Keep innovation modest and defensible.
- State which facts are directly verifiable, which are reasonable public-evidence inferences, and which depend on unavailable internal data.
- Name the official thesis form and analytical pattern.

Good: "The proposal states that Chapter 4 will use AHP to weight performance indicators and then compare pre/post evaluation results."

Weak: "The proposal promises to build a comprehensive model without specifying data or validation."

## Standalone Literature Review

Use a thematic structure:

- concept and measurement;
- influencing factors/mechanisms;
- methods/models;
- case/application;
- gaps and thesis position.

End each section with a short evaluation, not a list of authors.

## Deliverable Check

Before handing over:

- The topic, case, method, and data source are consistent.
- The schedule includes material collection and revision time.
- The references are all user-provided or verifiable from supplied materials.
