# Quality Checklist

Use this before major handoff and after revisions.

## Structure

- Topic, research question, case, theory, method, and chapters align.
- The thesis has one clear research question, not a broad topic or a list of issues.
- The introduction states the reality problem, key associated factor(s), consequence, and provisional answer/main claim.
- The official thesis form and analytical pattern are explicit.
- Chapter 2 theory is not a disconnected list.
- Case chapter supplies evidence for later diagnosis.
- Conclusion answers the introduction's research questions.

## Topic And Problem

- The problem is rooted in real professional practice, internship/work materials, public disclosures, or industry/regulatory events.
- The problem is visible in standard indicators or public/case evidence without invented concepts, reconstructed metrics, or data stretching.
- The narrative follows the data direction. Improvement is described as improvement before discussing sufficiency or hidden cost.
- The causal chain goes beyond identities and common sense.
- Core concepts have literature or management-practice roots.
- If the same sample has required three or more core-concept replacements, the topic/sample has been reconsidered rather than repackaged.

## MPAcc Practicality

- Accounting, audit, finance, management accounting, risk, internal control, or disclosure logic is visible.
- Recommendations are implementable by departments, systems, indicators, procedures, or accounting treatments.
- The thesis does not become a pure technology, law, management, or policy essay.
- The official thesis form is explicit: case analysis report, thematic research paper, survey report, scheme design, or product design.
- The analytical pattern is explicit: optimization, mechanism/effect, model/accounting treatment, event/spillover, configuration, staged path, or intelligent-technology application.
- Tool applications include industry-specific indicators, threshold basis, owner, data path, trigger action, and expected effect.

## Evidence

- Public data has source and date/page where available.
- Internal/interview/questionnaire data has acquisition explanation and desensitization.
- Missing data is marked with `〔待补：...〕`.
- Claims distinguish directly verifiable facts, reasonable public-evidence inference, and unverifiable internal-data limits.
- Horizontal/vertical comparison is used where feasible.
- Method-heavy claims pass `method-data-fit-guide.md`.
- Single-case or multi-case design is named and justified.
- Multi-case comparison is not overstated as strict causal identification.

## Citation

- Literature matrix and citation plan exist before full drafting.
- Each cited source was supplied by the user.
- Run `scripts/check_citations.py`.
- No invented authors, years, journals, or page numbers.

## Logic

- Every problem has evidence.
- Every cause explains a problem.
- Every solution targets a cause.
- Safeguards support implementation rather than repeat slogans.
- Avoid working-report style "many problems -> many causes -> many measures" unless the thesis explicitly selects one core relationship for deep proof.
- Decision-type solutions compare feasible alternatives and justify the selected plan.

## Style

- Flag boilerplate and generic "AI-like" paragraphs.
- Replace slogans with case facts, indicators, and reasoning.
- Avoid overclaiming method results.

Good review note: "Chapter 5.2 proposes a data platform, but Chapter 4 only diagnosed indicator-weight problems. Add evidence of data fragmentation or remove the platform recommendation."

Weak review note: "The thesis should be more profound and polished."
